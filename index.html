Jian Zou

Biomedical Data Science

Statistical learning methods for clinical and high-dimensional omics data.

I am Jian Zou (邹鉴), an Associate Professor at the Department of Statistics, School of Public Health, Chongqing Medical University. My research focuses on developing statistical learning methods that connect high-dimensional biomedical data with clinically meaningful questions in oncology and metabolic diseases.

Prior to CQMU, I completed my Ph.D. in Biostatistics at the School of Public Health, University of Pittsburgh in 2023, under the supervision of Dr. George C. Tseng, and co-advised by Dr. Steffi Oesterreich and Dr. Adrian Lee at the UPMC Hillman Cancer Center. I earned an M.S. in Biostatistics at the Mailman School of Public Health, Columbia University in 2019, and dual B.S. degrees in Biotechnology and Applied Chemistry from the School of Life Science, Central China Normal University in 2017.
